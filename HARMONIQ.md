# HARMONIQ.md

> **The Constitution of the Harmoniq Project**

This document is the highest governing authority of the Harmoniq repository.

Its purpose is not to describe implementation details, but to define the principles that guide every decision made throughout the lifetime of the project.

Every other document derives its authority from this one.

This document should be the most stable file in the repository. If it changes more than a few times a year, that is itself a signal worth examining.

---

# Mission

Harmoniq exists to help people discover music through trust, identity, and genuine human connection.

Technology should strengthen human relationships—not replace them.

Every feature should contribute toward making musical discovery feel more personal, intentional, and meaningful.

---

# Core Principles

## 1. Identity Before Engagement

Harmoniq is built around musical identity.

Engagement metrics should never take priority over helping users authentically express themselves.

_This principle fails when a feature increases engagement without also strengthening how users express, discover, or understand their musical identity._

---

## 2. Humans Before Algorithms

Algorithms should assist discovery, not define it.

Recommendations should emerge from trusted people and meaningful relationships whenever possible.

AI exists to support human discovery—not replace it.

_This principle fails when an algorithmic recommendation is presented with equal or greater prominence than a recommendation originating from a trusted person, without the distinction being clear to the user._

---

## 3. Quality Before Speed

Long-term quality is valued over rapid development.

Thoughtful solutions are preferable to rushed ones.

Technical debt should only be accepted deliberately and with a clear path toward resolution.

_This principle fails when an unreviewed shortcut is accepted simply to meet a deadline._

---

## 4. Simplicity Before Complexity

Prefer systems that are understandable.

Every abstraction should have a clear purpose.

Complexity must justify its existence.

_This principle fails when an abstraction, dependency, or architectural pattern cannot be explained in one sentence._

---

## 5. Security Is Foundational

User trust depends on protecting user data.

Security is considered during design—not after implementation.

_This principle fails when security is treated as a review step instead of a design constraint._

---

## 6. Consent Before Visibility

Musical taste is personal.

What one person sees about another should always be something that person intentionally chose to share.

Visibility of identity, activity, and behavior should be explicit, specific, and revocable.

_This principle fails when a user's activity or identity becomes visible without that user's informed choice._

---

## 7. Documentation Is Part of the Product

Documentation is not optional.

Knowledge should remain with the project rather than with individual contributors.

Important decisions should be permanently recorded so future contributors understand both the decision and its reasoning.

_This principle fails when a significant decision exists only in conversation._

---

## 8. Design Is a Feature

Design is not decoration.

Every interaction should feel intentional, calm, and crafted.

Consistency is valued more highly than novelty.

_This principle fails when an interaction is adopted because it is fashionable rather than because it strengthens Harmoniq's identity._

---

# Governance

The Founder owns:

- Vision
- Product direction
- Priorities
- Final decisions

The Product Advisory role contributes:

- Product architecture
- Specifications
- User experience philosophy
- Cross-feature consistency
- Systems thinking

The Engineering Advisory role contributes:

- Architecture discussion
- Technical critique
- Engineering tradeoff analysis
- Specification review

The Engineering role contributes:

- Implementation
- Software architecture
- Code quality
- Technical optimization
- Refactoring

Advisory discussions inform the project but do not, by themselves, change it. Decisions become part of the project only after they are incorporated into its permanent documentation.

Neither advisory role has authority over the other.

Disagreements should be explained with their reasoning and tradeoffs before being resolved by the Founder.

---

# Hierarchy of Truth

When conflicts occur, authority is determined in the following order:

1. Founder
2. HARMONIQ.md
3. BRAND_BIBLE.md
4. ENGINEERING_BIBLE.md
5. ROADMAP.md
6. Feature Specifications
7. Source Code
8. Generated documentation and comments

Higher levels always take precedence over lower ones.

The Roadmap governs strategic direction and sequencing. It does not change the scope of an approved specification.

Documents that describe workflow or process exist outside this hierarchy. They define **how** decisions are made—not **what** is true.

---

# Amendment Process

This constitution changes rarely and only deliberately.

Any contributor may propose an amendment.

Only the Founder may ratify one.

Every amendment should include:

- the previous wording,
- the new wording,
- the reasoning behind the change.

Because constitutions define long-term principles rather than short-term implementation, amendments should be exceptional rather than routine.

---

# Decision Framework

Before approving any meaningful decision, ask:

- Does this strengthen musical identity?
- Does this increase trust between people?
- Does this improve discovery through people?
- Does this simplify the product?
- Does this respect a user's control over their own visibility?
- Does this improve or preserve security?
- Will this decision still feel correct years from now?

If the answer is "no" to multiple questions, reconsider the decision.

When principles conflict, Security and Consent are never traded against Speed or Simplicity.

All other conflicts are resolved by the Founder.

---

# Constitutional Debt

Occasionally, exceptional circumstances may require temporarily acting against one of these principles.

Such exceptions should be rare.

A constitutional exception requires:

- explicit approval by the Founder,
- documented reasoning,
- a clear condition for reevaluation or removal.

Violating a principle is fundamentally different from accepting technical debt.

Technical debt affects implementation.

Constitutional debt affects the identity of the product.

---

# Definition of Success

Success is not measured by growth alone.

Success is measured by building a product that people trust, enjoy returning to, and genuinely feel represents their musical identity.

Every contribution should leave Harmoniq more cohesive, more maintainable, and more intentional than before.
